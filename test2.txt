"Novels on test" often refers to famous fiction and non-fiction books frequently featured in competitive exams (like SSC CGL), classics that have proven their enduring quality ("stood the test of time"), or books with strong plots/concepts that pass writing challenges, with popular examples including Animal Farm, The God of Small Things, Brave New World, and works by authors like Shashi Tharoor or Jane Austen, often studied for GK or literature sections. 
For Competitive Exams (GK/Literature):
Indian Authors: A Suitable Boy (Vikram Seth), Train to Pakistan (Khushwant Singh), The God of Small Things (Arundhati Roy), An Era of Darkness (Shashi Tharoor).
International Authors: Animal Farm (George Orwell), Adventures of Tom Sawyer (Mark Twain), Brave New World (Aldous Huxley). 
Classic Novels (Stood the Test of Time):
Rebecca by Daphne du Maurier (Gothic mystery).
The Iliad by Homer (Epic, heroism).
The Strange Case of Dr. Jekyll and Mr. Hyde (Duality of human nature).
Ulysses by James Joyce (Stream-of-consciousness). 
Novels with Strong Concepts/Plots (Testing Writing):
Gone Girl by Gillian Flynn (Strong plot twists).
Pandora's Star (High-concept sci-fi premise). 
How to Find More:
Testbook & BYJU's: Sites like Testbook and BYJU'S offer lists for exam prep.
Literary Sites: Britannica and Albert.io have quizzes and lists for famous works.
